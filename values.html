Our Values and Standards

At Revalen, our values are not aspirational — they are operational. They guide our decision-making, shape our partnerships, and define how we conduct ourselves in complex and high-risk environments.

Shared Values

Protect

We safeguard lives, livelihoods, data, and integrity — for our clients, partners, and affected communities.

Provide

We deliver practical solutions, meaningful analysis, and reliable capabilities under pressure.

Promote

We promote transparency, accountability, justice, and dignity — especially in places where these are absent or under threat.

Advance

We advance the mission, the mandate, and the moment — making sure our presence counts.

Human Standards

These are the standards we expect from ourselves and from those we work with — from contractors and commercial partners to field agents and senior advisors.

Dignity

  • Treat all people as equal in worth
  • Help others maintain dignity, regardless of their situation
  • Use language and behaviour that upholds respect

Truth

  • Challenge disinformation and reject clickbait
  • Do not amplify claims or material you cannot verify
  • Use evidence and logic, not popularity, to form conclusions

Restraint

  • Don't escalate a situation just because you can
  • Limit exposure, force, and harm to only what is necessary
  • Apply judgment, not just capability

Service

  • Serve others as well as your own mission
  • Remember that effectiveness includes empathy
  • Be useful — even when no one is watching

Justice

  • Work to address the causes of injustice, not just the symptoms
  • Support accountability for abuses of power
  • Uphold laws that protect people, not interests

Discretion

  • Respect privacy, need-to-know boundaries, and mission security
  • Be cautious with details that could endanger others
  • Don't confuse transparency with exposure

Objectivity

  • Separate your job from your bias
  • Report and assess facts, not ideology
  • Follow the evidence — not your instincts

Initiative

  • Don't wait to be told what needs to be done
  • Do the small things early to prevent bigger problems later
  • Keep moving the mission forward

Credibility

  • Be honest about what you know and what you don’t
  • Earn trust through competence and reliability
  • Protect the reputation of the mission, not just your own
